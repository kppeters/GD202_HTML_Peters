Encouraging Student Utility

D.H. Hill Library

Interview

One of the people that we have had the opportunity to interview was Camron Gonzales. He is a Junior and NC State studying Communications with a concentration in Media and is also minoring in Art & Design.

"I have a very busy, early morning schedule. I wake up, I go to work, and then I have class all the way up till 4:15 and in which case I’m usually done for the night. So usually I take that time to do work or anything like that. I occasionally visit D.H. Hill. I'm not there every day but I am there frequently enough."

Do you experience any problems with D.H. Hill?

Since I’m someone that goes alone to the library, I would definitely say that there’s an issue with individual study space. I believe that once you get up top to like the bookstack floors there’s these little booths but there’s not that many of them. And I think the second floor which is outside of the bookstack has like these little cool futuristic things you can recline into it and they look really comfy but there’s not that many of them. So definitely like individual study space would be really appreciated.

What resources have you used?

Definitely the printing. Mostly the printing. I come there and I print whatever assignments or readings that I print out if I need to. I think I’ve gone to the workshop to 3D print, but I don’t think I’ve ever actually 3D printed so definitely not that. Oh, I do use the technology loan. I’ve used it for a couple of projects that I’ve had to do. Renting out cameras, microphones, etc.

What are some tips you wish you knew?

What I wish I would’ve known was all the existing study spots because there are still a lot of study spots that I am finding that I never even knew were there that have apparently been there the entire time so you know maybe something that says like “hey, these are the areas you could study at, here are the areas you can look at the books,” such as the bookstacks, but definitely like knowing where study areas are.

Kali Peters - GD 202 Townscend Sec 01 - Spring 2020
