Encouraging Student Utility

D.H. Hill Library

Map

We observed and interviewed students who frequent D.H. Hill library to discover the ways they use the library’s resources and spaces. We find out that both students typically lead busy, studious lives as students and will frequent the library. During observations at the location, we find this to be typical behavior of the users there.

We found that a student’s intentions for coming to the library, or what they are trying to achieve during their visit, dictates whether or not they come individually or as a group and how they use the space. Many other users seemed to struggle with finding spaces that suited their needs. Other information gained in these interviews and observations suggests that only a few of the resources provided in the library are known of and used (printing, technology loan, study space reservations.) A survey we conducted shows that the users are not limited to students, but also professors, staff, and researchers. The main points that came up in the survey was that it was difficult to find spaces that worked, the appearance could be updated, and a new wayfinding system would be handy.

Kali Peters - GD 202 Townscend Sec 01 - Spring 2020
